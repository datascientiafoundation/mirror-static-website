English and a handful of other languages dominate the internet, but this is leaving indigenous cultures without a voice online. Now they are fighting to get their own languages on the web. <\/h4>\n\n\n\n

Imagine your favourite social media platform does not let you post in English. Now think of a keyboard that won\u2019t allow you to type in your own words. You would have two options: either switch to another language or remain digitally silent.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This is the reality for most people that speak indigenous languages and dialects.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

There are nearly\u00a07,000 languages and dialects<\/a>\u00a0in the world, yet only 7% are reflected in published online material, according to Whose knowledge?, a campaign that aims to\u00a0make visible the knowledge of marginalised communities online<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
